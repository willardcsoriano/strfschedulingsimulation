SRTF Scheduling Simulation

Column Explanations

  • Job: Unique identifier for each job/process in the simulation.
  • Arrival Time: Time at which each job arrives in the system. In this simulation, jobs arrive at random times between 1 and 7.
  • Burst Time: Total time required for each job to complete execution, randomly assigned between 1 and 10.
  • Completion Time: Time at which each job finishes its execution. Calculated as:
    Completion Time = Turnaround Time + Arrival Time
  • Turnaround Time: Total time taken from the arrival of the job to its completion. Calculated as:
    Turnaround Time = Completion Time - Arrival Time
  • Waiting Time: Total time a job spends waiting in the queue to be executed. Calculated as:
    Waiting Time = Turnaround Time - Burst Time

SRTF Scheduling Algorithm Explanation

The Shortest-Remaining-Time-First (SRTF) scheduling algorithm is a preemptive version of the Shortest Job Next (SJN) scheduling. It always selects the job with the shortest remaining time to execute next. If a new job arrives with a shorter burst time than the remaining time of the current job, the current job is preempted, and the new job is executed.

Steps in the Algorithm:

  1. Initialization: At time 0, all jobs arrive in the system.
  2. Job Selection: The algorithm selects the job with the shortest remaining burst time.
  3. Execution: The selected job is executed for one time unit.
  4. Preemption Check: If a new job arrives with a shorter burst time, it preempts the current job.
  5. Completion Check: If a job completes its execution, its completion time is recorded.
  6. Repeat: Steps 2-5 are repeated until all jobs are completed.

This approach ensures that the job with the shortest remaining time gets executed first, minimizing the waiting time and improving the overall efficiency of the system.
